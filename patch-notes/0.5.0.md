Escaping the grove is a team effort now. The Heartwood Gate is not the finish line; the platform
beyond it is, and you all have to stand on it together while the grove decides whether to let you go.

- **New: a co-op extraction.** Lighting the last shrine opens the gate as before, but reaching the
  exit is no longer an every-survivor-for-themselves dash. The way out is a glowing platform past the
  gate, and you escape only by holding it together: every survivor who needs to be there has to be
  standing on the platform at the same time, and a timer counts down while you hold it. Step off, or get
  dragged into the roots, and the timer resets.
- **New: difficulty changes who has to make it.** On Amateur and Hardcore only the survivors still
  on their feet need to reach the platform; a downed teammate does not strand the rest of you. On
  Nightmare it is leave-no-one-behind: a cocooned teammate has to be freed and brought to the platform
  before the grove will release any of you (a survivor who is gone for good will not trap the others).
  The hold is quickest on Amateur and longest on Hardcore.
- **New: the way out is a frozen ritual circle.** The exit is a proper ruined circle of dark stone and
  cold crystal rising out of the blight, crowned with glowing Moonbloom, not a patch of light on the
  ground, so you can find it and rally on it. It appears when the last shrine is lit (it is not standing
  there from the start of the night), and the old glowing gate archway is gone.
- **New: an extraction readout.** While the gate is open, the HUD shows how many of you are on the
  platform and how long is left on the hold, so the party can see at a glance who still has to make it.

The hunters got scarier. They watch you with glowing eyes from the dark, laugh when they
catch you out, and their blows hit far harder than before.

- **New: glowing-eyed hunters.** The Blighted Kweebec now wears a corrupted, darkened look and
  stares out of the night with glowing eyes, so you can feel one watching before you see the rest
  of it.
- **New: the hunters snicker.** When a hunter is close, or when it lands a hit on you, it has a
  chance to let out a low, eerie cackle from its own direction, a taunting "I see you" cue layered
  over the heartbeat and whispers.
- **New: far more devastating attacks.** A hunter's hit now strikes for much more than before and
  launches you back, and how hard each one hits depends on what kind it is: the charging Lunger
  hits hardest, the ranged Spitter the softest, with the Stalker and Ambusher in between.

Choosing your descent is its own screen now. Tell the Grove Warden a difficulty and you decide how
you face it: with anyone who answers the call, with your own party, or alone.

- **New: the Enter the Nightmare screen.** Picking a difficulty no longer drops you straight into a
  queue. It opens a screen where you choose how to play: Public (brave the grove with whoever else
  is waiting), Party (gather your own survivors first), or Alone (step into the dark by yourself,
  right now). Each is a card with its own icon.
- **New: the gathering counts down on screen.** Once you are waiting, the screen shows who is with
  you and a launch timer that ticks down live as the grove pulls you in, instead of a static notice.
- **New: parties carry your difficulty.** When you queue as a party, it now enters the difficulty you
  chose, and the party can stay an open gathering that fills with strangers or seal itself to your
  own members.
- **New: which ways you can enter are per-difficulty.** Each difficulty decides on its own which of
  Public, Party, and Alone it offers, so a harder night can ask you to bring company.

The grove has new things to throw. Alongside the Moonbloom you already lob to stun a hunter, three
more glowing mushrooms can be gathered and hurled, each bursting differently when it lands.

- **New: throw glowing mushrooms to fight back.** Three new thrown mushrooms join Moonbloom: a Gustbloom
  that knocks whatever it hits flying (buy yourself room to run), a Mirebloom that leaves hunters
  slogging through a slowing haze, and an Emberbloom that bursts for real damage.
- **New: crack the deep crypt for an arsenal.** The chest deep in the large crypt no longer holds odds
  and ends; it is now a cache of the new throwing mushrooms, so braving the descent arms you with a
  spread of all three.
- **New: blast the Warden with Emberblooms.** When the corrupted Warden rises at the gate, clusters of
  Emberbloom sprout around it and keep regrowing, so you can gather and hurl them at the boss to wear
  down something that would otherwise outlast you. (Emberblooms never hurt a fellow survivor.)

This is an in-development build. The new hunter look, snicker cues, attack tuning, the Enter
the Nightmare screen, and the new throwing mushrooms are pending live playtest; the server build is green.
